THE KOLLEKTIV PROGRAM

★ Sechenov's Grand Vision ★

In an alternate timeline, the Soviet Union achieved technological supremacy through the discovery of Polymer — a miraculous substance that could interface directly with the human neural network. Professor Dmitry Sechenov, visionary scientist and hero of the state, created a utopia where robots served humanity and labor was abolished.

But on the day of Kollektiv 2.0's launch — the system that would network every citizen's mind into a perfect collective consciousness — something went terribly wrong. The robots turned hostile. The facility locked down. And the dream of a perfect Soviet future became a waking nightmare.

POLYMER TECHNOLOGY

  • Neural-interface biomaterial discovered 1936
  • Enables direct mind-machine communication
  • Powers all robotic systems in Facility 3826
  • Kollektiv network: mass neural synchronization
  • Side effects: mutation, loss of autonomy, death

Polymer Glove (P-9)

DESIGNATION: P-9/TELEKINESIS

A weaponized glove that channels Polymer energy through the user's nervous system. Grants telekinetic abilities, cryogenic blasts, electrical discharge, and polymeric shield generation. The glove bonds permanently with the host's neural pathways — removal is not recommended.

CHAR-les

DESIGNATION: AI-COMPANION/NEURAL

An AI consciousness implanted directly into the protagonist's glove. CHAR-les provides tactical analysis, sarcastic commentary, and moral guidance — though his true loyalties remain questionable. His neural presence is a constant companion in the facility's horrors.

Neuropolymer Network

DESIGNATION: KOLLEKTIV/BACKBONE

The backbone infrastructure of the Kollektiv system. A vast web of Polymer conduits running through every wall, floor, and ceiling of Facility 3826. It carries neural signals, powers robots, and was designed to unify all Soviet citizens into a single collective consciousness.

CHRONOLOGY

★ Key Events ★
1936

Discovery of Polymer

Soviet scientists discover a biomechanical substance in the Ural mountains capable of interfacing with organic neural tissue. Sechenov, then a young researcher, recognizes its transformative potential.

1941

Sechenov's Rise

Polymer-powered weapons turn the tide of the Great Patriotic War. Sechenov is awarded Hero of the Soviet Union and given unlimited resources to develop the technology further.

1948

Facility 3826 Established

Construction of the massive underground-and-surface research complex begins. It will house thousands of scientists, workers, and the largest concentration of robots in the Soviet Union.

1951

Kollektiv 1.0 Launch

The first-generation neural network goes online, connecting all robots to a central command system. Productivity increases 400%. The Soviet Union declares a new era of prosperity.

1955

Kollektiv 2.0 — The Incident

On launch day, the upgraded system — designed to network human minds into the collective — catastrophically fails. Every robot in the facility turns hostile simultaneously. Communications are cut. The facility becomes a sealed tomb of chrome and blood.
