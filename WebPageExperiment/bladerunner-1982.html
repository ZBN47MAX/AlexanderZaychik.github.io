THE WORLD

Setting

November 2019, Los Angeles. A perpetually dark, rain-soaked megalopolis choked with industrial smog. Massive off-world colonies have drained Earth of its best and brightest, leaving behind a dense, polyglot underclass living in the shadow of corporate monoliths.

Replicants

The Tyrell Corporation advances replicant evolution — bioengineered beings virtually identical to humans. Nexus-6 models possess superior strength and intelligence but are given a four-year lifespan as a failsafe. After a bloody mutiny off-world, replicants are declared illegal on Earth. Special police operatives — Blade Runners — are tasked with hunting and 'retiring' them.

Deckard

Rick Deckard, ex-Blade Runner, is pulled out of retirement by Captain Bryant to track down four escaped Nexus-6 replicants who have hijacked a shuttle and returned to Earth illegally, seeking their maker — Eldon Tyrell — to extend their rapidly expiring lifespans.

Tyrell Corporation

‘More Human Than Human’ is their motto. The Tyrell Corporation’s headquarters — a colossal golden pyramid dominating the LA skyline — represents the pinnacle of corporate power. Dr. Eldon Tyrell, the reclusive genius founder, lives in opulent solitude at the pyramid’s apex, playing god with engineered life.

KEY LOCATIONS

Tyrell Pyramid
Colossal golden ziggurat rising above the smog. Corporate headquarters and Tyrell's private residence. Owl-filled chambers of cold opulence.
Bradbury Building
J.F. Sebastian's decaying Victorian apartment building. Ornate ironwork, broken skylights, rain pouring through. Home to Sebastian's handmade genetic toys.
Deckard's Apartment
97th floor. Blue-lit brutalist interior. Piano, bourbon, and photographs. The Esper machine. Window views of endless rain and city lights.
Snake Pit / Taffey Lewis's
Neon-drenched nightclub in the city's underbelly. Where Zhora performs with her synthetic snake. Smoke, noise, sweat, and neon reflections on wet floors.
Animoid Row
Open-air market for artificial animals. Neon-lit stalls, vendors shouting in Cityspeak. Where Deckard traces the scale to the snake maker.
